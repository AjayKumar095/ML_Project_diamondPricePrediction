Diamond Facts

Diamonds are not only the hardest substance on Earth but also hold a rich history. The world's largest diamond, the Cullinan, weighed an astounding 3,106 carats before being cut into smaller gems. Diamonds are formed deep within the Earth's mantle over millions of years under extreme pressure and high temperatures. The Hope Diamond, one of the most famous diamonds in the world, is said to carry a curse. Throughout history, diamonds have been associated with strength, wealth, and everlasting love. The Koh-i-Noor, a famous diamond, has a storied past, believed to have brought misfortune to some of its owners.

One of the rarest diamonds is the Blue Moon. This exquisite diamond was sold for a record-breaking $48.5 million at an auction. Another exceptional diamond is the Moussaieff Red, known for its deep red color. The Dresden Green Diamond, with its distinctive green hue, is displayed at the Dresden Castle in Germany. These rare gems showcase the diversity and beauty found in the world of diamonds.
